Understanding Inputs, Outputs, and Constraints: The Foundation of Problem Solving

Hello there! I’m so happy you are joining me at the Kselis Institute. Today, we are going to learn a super important way to look at *any* problem—no matter how big or small. We are going to talk about three simple but powerful ideas: Inputs, Outputs, and Constraints. Understanding these three things is like having a secret map that helps you plan anything successfully!

What are Inputs? (What You Start With)

Think of inputs as all the materials, resources, information, or energy you start with before you begin any task...

What are Outputs? (What You Want to Achieve)

The output is simply the final result, the goal, or what you are trying to create by using your inputs...

What are Constraints? (The Limitations)

Constraints are the rules, limitations, restrictions, or boundaries you have to work within...

Putting It All Together: The Relationship

Inputs, Outputs, and Constraints work together in a cycle...

Analogy: Think about baking a cake. Your **Inputs** are the ingredients (flour, sugar, eggs). Your desired **Output** is the finished cake...

Try it yourself!

Run the code below to see how constraints affect the output of planning a party. Try changing the input budget to see what happens!

# 1. Inputs (What we have)
SET max_budget TO 500
SET cost_per_guest TO 20

# We can ask the user for an input!
SET guests TO INPUT_NUM("How many guests are you inviting?")

# Calculate total needed
SET total_cost TO guests * cost_per_guest

# 2. Constraints (The limits we must check)
IF total_cost > max_budget THEN
    # 3. Output (Failure state)
    PRINT "Constraint Failed: The party costs " + TO_STRING(total_cost) + " but you only have " + TO_STRING(max_budget)
ELSE
    # 3. Output (Success state)
    PRINT "Output Achieved: Party is successfully planned!"
    PRINT "You have " + TO_STRING(max_budget - total_cost) + " left over."
ENDIF

So, whenever you face a problem—whether it’s organizing a big family trip or managing your monthly finances—stop and ask yourself these three questions: What do I have? What do I want? And what are my limits? By answering these questions clearly, you gain the power to solve almost anything! Keep practicing this mindset, and you will become a master problem-solver!
